Territory

Levi POV

Levi didn’t say anything at the bar.

That alone should have been warning enough.

The place was loud — glasses clinking, laughter spilling over itself, music too confident for its own good. Friends crowded around the table, half-drunk and nostalgic, stories tumbling out without much thought.

Someone mentioned Eren’s name.
Someone else mentioned before.

“Oh yeah,” one of them laughed, “remember when Eren was seeing that guy back then? God, that was a mess.”

The sentence was careless.
The tone light.

Levi’s grip tightened around his glass.

Eren didn’t look at him. He didn’t need to. Levi could feel the shift in his posture anyway — the instinctive awareness, the quiet I know between them. Eren deflected easily, joked it away, changed the subject like he always did.

Levi stayed silent.

This wasn’t the place.
These weren’t the people.
And Eren wasn’t something to be claimed out loud like territory on a map.

Still — the thought lodged itself deep, sharp and unwelcome.

Someone else had held him.
Someone else had been trusted with his laughter, his warmth, his softness.

Levi hated how much it unsettled him.

He trusted Eren. Completely.
That wasn’t the issue.

The issue was older than reason. Deeper than logic.

The idea that something so vital to him had ever belonged to someone else — even briefly — made his chest ache in a way he didn’t have words for.

They left early.

At home, the quiet pressed in immediately — familiar, safe. Coats discarded, shoes kicked aside. The world shrank to walls and warm light.

Levi busied himself with nothing.
Eren watched him for a minute.

Then, gently, “You’ve been quiet.”

Levi didn’t answer.

Eren stepped closer. “Levi.”

He exhaled slowly, jaw tight. “I know it’s stupid.”

Eren didn’t interrupt.

“I know you didn’t do anything wrong,” Levi continued, voice low. “I know you’re with me. I trust you. This isn’t—” He paused, frustrated. “It’s not about logic.”

Eren nodded, expression calm, attentive.

“It just—” Levi swallowed. “I don’t like thinking about someone else having you. Having that version of you. I don’t like that they existed in a place I care about.”

He looked away, annoyed with himself. “I know you don’t belong to me like an object. I know that. But you’re… mine. And I’m yours. And sometimes my head doesn’t know how to shut up about it.”

For a moment, there was only silence.

Then Eren stepped in front of him, close enough that Levi could feel his warmth.

“You’re not stupid,” Eren said softly. “You’re human.”

Levi scoffed. “Debatable.”

Eren smiled faintly. “I don’t mind that you feel things deeply. I never have.” He lifted a hand, hesitant only for a second, then cupped the side of Levi’s face. “Nothing from before takes anything away from us. From you.”

Levi’s eyes flickered up.

“I chose you,” Eren said. “I choose you. Every day.”

The tension in Levi’s chest cracked, slow and painful, like ice thawing.

“I know,” he muttered.

“And,” Eren added, amused, “for the record — you’re not the only territorial one here.”

That earned a huff of reluctant laughter.

Before Levi could look away, Eren leaned in.

It wasn’t rushed.
It wasn’t gentle in the way apologies were.

Eren’s hand slid to the back of Levi’s neck, fingers firm, grounding — unmistakably there. The kiss was brief but deliberate, pressed with intent rather than softness, as if Eren were reminding him of something fundamental.

Mine.

Levi felt it all the way down to his spine.

When Eren pulled back, he didn’t move far. Their foreheads nearly touched. Levi opened his eyes and met Eren’s gaze, close enough to see the warmth there, steady and unwavering.

It settled him instantly.

That warmth — that quiet certainty — spread through his chest, easing the ache, silencing the noise. A power Eren had over him that Levi never fought. Never wanted to.

He exhaled, slow, controlled.

“Come on,” Eren said. “You’ve had a long night.”

Levi barely had time to react before Eren wrapped him up in a blanket on the couch, methodical and unapologetic, folding him in until there was nowhere else to go.

“A burrito?” Levi deadpanned.

“A very grumpy one.”

Eren settled behind him, arms secure, chin resting lightly against Levi’s head. Warm. Steady. Unmoving.

Levi let himself sink back.

The world went quiet.

“It’s okay,” Eren murmured. “I’ve got you.”

Levi closed his eyes.

He always had.
