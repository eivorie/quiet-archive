This exists because you do.

Some stories don’t belong in a chat history.
They deserve a place to stay.

My love, this is the part of the story I never really tell — the raw version, the one that strips me down to bone. The one that shows you exactly what you are to me.

I should start in 2016.
Two characters, two cosplays — you as Eren, me as Levi — pretending to speak while two real people behind the screens brushed against each other's existence.
We barely talked. Weeks, maybe. Then silence.
But something in me marked you anyway.

For years you were a ghost orbiting my life — a name I couldn’t forget, a face I saw in passing, a presence that felt too familiar for no reason.
I watched you change, grow, break a little, rise again, all through stories and tiny updates that weren’t meant for me.

I screenshotted you sometimes. I still don’t know why.
You asked for a drawing once. You still don’t know why.

And now we laugh, calling ourselves insane, cursed, completely gone — but deep down we both feel the truth: something was already pulling us toward each other long before we were ready.

March of last year — your birthday.
A message I shouldn’t have cared about, but did.
I wrote you something simple, and somehow you opened up to me like someone who’d always known where to place his heart.

Then you vanished.
For months.
And I lost my mind quietly, politely, without letting a single scream escape.

I tried to reach you without reaching out.
Posted things that reminded me of you. Things I would never have posted for anyone else.
Tiny breadcrumbs dropped in your direction because I didn’t know how to say:
"Where did you go? I wasn’t done with you."

Then August 2nd.
Out of nowhere:
“How are you?”

My world ruptured right there.

We talked. God, we talked.
And everything clicked with a violence that felt destined.
Like I recognized you from a life I never lived.
Like my soul leaned into yours before I understood what was happening.

We killed our routines.
We ignored time zones.
Nights melted into mornings because your presence mattered more than sleep, more than reason, more than anything else.

And whenever we slipped into our Ereri RP, something inside me cracked open — like fiction was only a cover for something real, something old, something ours.

I fell in love with you in days.
Days.
And I don’t care how insane that sounds — nothing about you has ever obeyed logic anyway.

The jealousy, the arguments, the panic —
I didn’t know I could feel like that.
You woke nerves in me I thought were long dead.
You made me furious, desperate, alive.

Then came your voice.
Your first audio.
Meu amor, I swear something in me died and was reborn in the same breath.
You were real.
And I was finished.

We talked about everything — childhood, wounds, countries, dreams, stupid stories, deep fears.
We built a private universe between two continents and pretended it was normal.

September 19th — our “drink together.”
A fake date that wasn’t fake at all.
You thought I forgot.
I couldn’t have forgotten if I tried.

And then you said it: you loved me.
I said it back.
We drowned in it until the night ended.

Then came those three days you had to leave.
Three days where my brain tore me apart, whispering that you’d come back different.
That I’d lose you before I could even hold you properly.

So I wrote.
Hour by hour.
Pages of everything I felt, everything I feared, everything I wanted.
Because if I didn’t write, I would have lost my mind.

And every night you asked for it.
Every night you read it.
Every night you showed me that my heart wasn’t shouting into a void — it was speaking to you.

You wanted to know everything about me.
My thoughts, my habits, my tics, my moods, my annoyances, my joys.
You studied me like someone who wasn’t planning to let go.

And that’s when I knew:
I wasn’t an option. I was chosen.

You are jealous, possessive, intense — and so am I.
We love with our teeth, our fire, our hunger.
We love like it’s instinct, like it’s inevitable.
And for the first time in my life, that kind of madness feels safe.

Sometimes I’m angry at 2016.
We should’ve found each other sooner.
You say you should’ve been my first, my only — and God, I feel the same.

Because no one, absolutely no one, has ever touched me the way you do.
Not in heart, not in mind, not in soul.

I think about you constantly.
When we talk.
When we don’t talk.
When we call.
When we hang up.
You’re in my bloodstream now.
In my breath.
In every stupid heartbeat.

We talk about the future —
where we’ll live, the coffee shop, the animals, the quiet routines, the loud nights, all the versions of us still waiting.

And here is the part I’ve tried to hide, the truth that scares me because of how absolute it feels:

I didn’t fall for you.
I crashed into you.
I shattered on impact.
And every piece of me stayed wanting only you.

It was always you.
In every timeline, in every universe, in every life we missed and will miss — it was always you.

My love isn’t gentle.
It’s raw, it’s burning, it’s brutal.
And it belongs to you.

And now… now we’re here.
In this strange, impossible place where everything I swore I’d never do for anyone has become the most natural thing with you.

I used to hate sending audios.
I used to avoid letting people hear my voice — avoid giving them that intimacy, that access.

But with you?
If I go through a whole day without speaking to you — without hearing your voice — something in me goes hollow.
It feels wrong.
Incomplete.
Like I’m walking around missing a piece of myself.

I need you now in ways I don’t even know how to hide anymore.
I need your voice, your breath, your laugh, the way you say my name like it belongs to you.
I need your good mornings, your tired good nights, the softness you don’t even notice you give me.

I love you more every day — in ways that scare me, in ways that steady me, in ways that make me want to rewrite my whole life just to fit you into every moment I missed.
I want more of you.
More of your presence, your voice, the future we’re building without even meaning to.

Loving you didn’t just change me — it rewired me.
And I don’t ever want to be who I was before you.

There has never been — and will never be — anyone but you.
